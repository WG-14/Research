Do not assume the diagnosis is fully correct. Treat the following as hypotheses and either confirm, weaken, or reject them with code evidence.

Hypothesis A:
The problem is not absence of a validation generator, but insufficient authority of the validation generator relative to profile/promotion verification. There may be no single experiment lineage ledger that proves the operator went through:
`research-backtest → research-walk-forward → research-promote-candidate → profile-generate → profile-verify → paper validation evidence → profile-promote`.

Hypothesis B:
There may be a semantic gap between research simulation decisions and runtime/paper decisions. Research may be pure replay/simulation, while live/paper execution may use a different strategy path, broker/order lifecycle, recovery behavior, or lot-native SELL authority. If so, decision equivalence should become a mandatory promotion/transition evidence gate, not merely documentation or optional tooling.

Hypothesis C:
The acceptance gate may have a solid scientific skeleton, but may not sufficiently defend against experiment-family-level overfitting, repeated final-holdout reuse, multiple-testing, or researcher degrees of freedom.

Hypothesis D:
Artifact hashes may be strong against tampering, but may not be sufficient to prove reproducibility. A JSON hash proves “this artifact did not change,” not necessarily “this artifact can be regenerated from the same code, dataset, manifest, command args, and calibration inputs.”
